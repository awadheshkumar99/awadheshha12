MECHANICAL

Mechanical Engineering: Deals with the design and manufacturing of mechanical systems, ranging from machines and engines to HVAC systems. Incorporates principles of materials science, thermodynamics, and mechanics.

CHEMICAL

Chemical Engineering: Applies principles of chemistry, physics, and mathematics to design and optimize processes for large-scale production. Involves areas such as chemical processing, material synthesis, and environmental engineering.

INDUSTRIAL

Aims to optimize complex systems and processes for improved efficiency and productivity. Involves areas such as operations research, supply chain management, and quality control.

SOFTWARE

Concentrates on the systematic design, development, testing, and maintenance of software. Involves programming, software architecture, and project management.

ELECTRICAL

Electrical Engineering: Concentrates on the study of electrical systems, electronics, and electromagnetism. Involves designing and developing electrical circuits, systems, and devices.

AEROSPACE

Aerospace Engineering: Specializes in the design, development, and testing of aircraft and spacecraft. Integrates principles from mechanical and electrical engineering, as well as aerodynamics.
